# Sample Rheumatology Board-Style Question: Lupus

## Clinical Vignette

A 27-year-old woman presents with fatigue, inflammatory joint pain, photosensitive rash, and oral ulcers. Laboratory testing shows positive ANA, elevated anti-dsDNA antibodies, low complement levels, and proteinuria.

## Question Stem

Which additional evaluation is most important to determine disease severity and guide treatment?

## Answer Choices

A. Repeat ANA testing  
B. Kidney biopsy  
C. Skin biopsy of normal-appearing skin  
D. HLA-B27 testing  
E. Serum uric acid level  

## Correct Answer

B. Kidney biopsy

## Explanation

In a patient with systemic lupus erythematosus and evidence of renal involvement, kidney biopsy is important to classify lupus nephritis, assess activity and chronicity, and guide treatment intensity.

## Teaching Point

Proteinuria in SLE should prompt evaluation for lupus nephritis. Kidney biopsy helps determine class, prognosis, and treatment strategy.
